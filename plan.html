Find a hackathon

Start by finding a hackathon that aligns with your interests and skills. You can search online for upcoming hackathons or look for local events through your university, community groups, or industry associations.

Team Registration

Start by finding a hackathon that aligns with your interests and skills. You can search online for upcoming hackathons or look for local events through your university, community groups, or industry associations.

3

Project Planning & Execution

Once you have your team in place, you'll need to plan and execute your project. This will involve brainstorming ideas, deciding on a project that you can realistically complete within the timeframe of the hackathon, and dividing the work among team members. Make sure to stay organized and communicate effectively with your team throughout the process.

4

Project Presentation

At the end of the hackathon, you'll need to present your project to the judges. Make sure to practice your presentation beforehand, and be prepared to answer any questions that the judges may have. Remember to focus on the key features of your project and how they address the problem you are trying to solve. Also, make sure to showcase any unique or innovative aspects of your project.
